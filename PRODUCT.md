# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Operations teams and small business owners managing inventory, customers, and orders daily. They interact with the system on desktop and mobile, performing CRUD operations across products, customers, and orders. The primary workflow is scanning dashboards, managing stock levels, and processing orders quickly. Speed and reliability matter more than visual novelty.

## Product Purpose

An inventory and order management system that gives teams a single source of truth for products, customers, and order fulfillment. Success means fast, error-free inventory operations with clear visibility into stock levels and order status.

## Brand Personality

Clean, efficient, trustworthy. The interface should feel like a well-organized workspace: everything in its place, nothing decorative, every element earns its screen space. Confidence through restraint, not flash.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / AI slop: cream backgrounds, gradient text, identical card grids, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows, numbered section markers (01 / 02 / 03), glassmorphism used decoratively
- Bouncy animations, elastic easing, decorative motion
- Overly warm/saturated body backgrounds (sand, cream, parchment tones)
- Hero-metric templates (big number + small label + gradient accent)
- Nested cards

## Design Principles

1. **Earn the space** — every element must serve a functional purpose; decorative elements are waste
2. **Speed is a feature** — visual feedback must be instant; motion reinforces responsiveness, not decoration
3. **Data speaks first** — let the information be the visual hierarchy; don't compete with content
4. **Restraint as identity** — the quiet confidence of a tool that works, not one that performs
5. **Consistent by default** — shared patterns across all surfaces; deviation requires a reason

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA compliance. Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements. Semantic HTML. Sufficient contrast for data-heavy tables and dashboards. No formal WCAG AAA requirements, but aim for strong contrast ratios on body text and interactive elements.
